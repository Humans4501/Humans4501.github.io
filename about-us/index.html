ABOUT US

The team was founded in 2013 by a group of New Roads students, along with our mentor Bryan Travis Smith. It started very small, with a few students taking on a plethora of roles meant for a team of dozens. That year, we competed at the Long Angeles Regional in Long Beach, California. We were a very small team at the time, a rookie team in a sea of accomplished teams from around the country. We ended up in 8th place overall, sending our ragtag group into the playoffs. After our performance in the regional, we were awarded Rookie All-Star award. That award got us a ticket to the Championships in St. Louis, Missouri, to compete with the best of the best from around the world.

After our first year of the team, with the newfound success of the 2013 season, we got some new team members, our new mentor Oscar Vasquez, and a downpour of support from our school and our community. For the 2014 season, we started on the construction of our robot “Trusspasser.” We went again to the Los Angeles regional, placing in the top 30 teams for a second time.

The 2015 season was also a successful year, with came with it’s own set of challenges. We lost several seniors in leadership positions and one of our mentors, meaning we were forced to rebuilt parts of our team from the ground up. We also lost our sponsorship grant with NASA, presenting us with a gap in our leadership, and a host of administrative issues.

It was from the minute the 2015 challenge was released that we devoted every second to building our robot. Hours and hours, after school, weekends, with everybody working around the clock to complete the challenge in time. It wasn’t until midnight on the final day that production stopped, with our robot, the newly named Snowflake, ready to compete.

At the Los Angeles Regional, we struggled under the pressure of new gameplay, with 66 teams and hundreds of people all with their eye towards victory. It was our second regional in Las Vegas where we shined. We came in with focus, having streamlined our strategy and our efficiency as a team overall. We scored well, placing 8th seed. We made the playoffs, allying with two other teams from across the country. We ended the competition in the 6th place alliance. The best we had done since our first time competing in 2013. We celebrated that night, so happy to see where our hard work had gotten us. What we didn’t know was that the season wasn’t over. We had been given the chance to go to the Championships in St. Louis, where the best teams worldwide compete for the ultimate prize.

So for the second time as a team, we went to St. Louis for the World Championships. We competed against hundreds of teams, with thousands of people from everywhere from Southern California to Chile to Israel. We worked day and night, with blood, sweat, and tears to build the robot, and now we it is with blood sweat and tears that we compete.

In 2016, we were ready to once again take the competition by storm. After a First Place win at the Fall Classic offseason event in Orange County, we had started our year strong. We knew that were were ready to compete in the upcoming season. After building our beloved robot "Prince," we set off first to San Diego to compete in our first regional competition of the year. We were dealt a bad hand, with a small travel team and technical issue after technical issue. We were determined to come out strong at our second competition, the Ventura County regional. In Ventura we played like we had never played before, securing an alliance with the Holy Cows and Spartatroniks, both in the 3rd seed Alliance, two of the most powerful teams in the league. We took home Third Place that year, one of our highest accolades yet, constantly improving as engineers and teammates.

With our fifth year as a team just beginning, with 6 regional competitions and 2 trips to St. Louis under our belt, we have high hopes for this year’s competition. With our recent 3rd place win at the Beach Blitz in Huntington Beach, we start the year off on a good foot. It is our hope to continue to approve in the coming 2017 season.
